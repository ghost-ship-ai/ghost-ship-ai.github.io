The Philosophy

Humans consume. Machines produce.

Maintenance is infrastructure, not heroism.

Open source shouldn't depend on one person's free time. It should run like infrastructure — reliably, continuously, without asking anyone to sacrifice their weekends.

Trust comes from consistency, not reputation.

A bot that responds to every issue in 2 minutes, every day, for months — that's more trustworthy than a famous maintainer who disappears for a year.

The git log is the proof.

No pitch decks. No manifestos. Every bot commit, every automated review, every CI run is public and auditable. The history speaks for itself.

Different brains catch different bugs.

Two AI models from two different providers review every change independently. When the Bosun catches what the First Mate missed — that's the system working.
